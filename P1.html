Project Idea

Our project idea is a user-friendly grocery management app aimed towards individuals with special dietary needs or restrictions. These users may include individuals with Diabetes, Celiac Disease, or those on special diets such as Vegan or Keto. The app will contain all the functionalities expected from a grocery management app such as the ability to select and enter grocery items, adjust quantities, track expiration dates and sync with other household members’ app. However, as mentioned above, our app will have a large focus on food items as our goal is to provide an effective way for users with dietary restrictions to plan, track and shop for groceries.

Furthermore, another functionality our app will have is a section where there will be recipe suggestions based on the current food items present at home. These recommended recipes will take into account the medical conditions and special diets which the user has identified and will suggest recipes popular with those who share those dietary restrictions. We expect the users of our app to be actively involved in refining the recommendations the app provides by using the “thumbs up” or “thumbs down” feature to indicate whether they would like to see more of this item or not. This ensure that the suggestions provided to the user are finely tuned to the specific needs and preferences of that user. A key part of these recommendations will be replacement items for a specified product which serves as alternatives that conform to the dietary restriction identified by the user. For example, if a user follows a vegan diet then searching for milk will result in suggestions for almond milk and soy milk as a replacement so that users can look for these items the next time they shop. We will combine all the user generated data with data from popular food and health websites to enable the app to provide accurate recommendations for specific food items and recipes which are popular with individuals with the same dietary restrictions.

Lastly, our app will also have the capability to link and partner with various grocery stores. We will be obtaining the ingredients and nutrition facts from the information provided on the USDA’s National Nutrient Database. All of this collected data will be used to support customized restrictions set by the users and the app will send an alert if any item contains a restricted ingredient. Future functionalities of the app may include the ability to order groceries from the participating stores directly from the mobile app.

Users

Our app can be used by any individual who needs an efficient grocery management app, however, our main user base will consist of teens and adults who have a specific medical condition or follow a special diet. This app is a tool designed to help these individuals who have dietary restrictions to effectively manage their groceries as well as plan, track and shop for grocery items that take into account the user’s special diet and personal food preferences. No special training or experience would be required to use this app as it would operate like other similar grocery management apps and will be intuitive to use as well as user-friendly.

Stakeholders

Our users are the main stakeholders, however, some other stakeholders may include doctors, nutritionists and dieticians who might want to suggest our app as a tool to help their patients and clients.

User Research Methods

For our project we chose the Secondary Research and the Persona IDEO methods. These methods complement each other because the Secondary Research helps inform our choice of Personas. By doing Secondary Research we can determine the different types of food restrictions our users may follow. We can also better understand the popularity of different food restrictions and the demographics of a typical user following that food restriction.

Health Aspect

For people who have certain diseases, they must keep an eye on specific food since certain food might be risky to their lives. This includes people having food allergies and other healthy issues such as heart disease, high blood pressure, high blood sugar, high blood lipid, asthma, diabetic and etc.

Food Allergies

The most common individual food allergies include those to peanuts, tree nuts (walnuts, pecans etc), fish, shellfish, eggs, milk, soy, corn and wheat.

Diabetics

For people who have diabetics, Insulin is needed to help cells take in the glucose. They need to pay special attention on the intake of carbohydrate-containing foods. A planned diabetic diet should help to schedule carbohydrate intake so that the cells can get the glucose that they need, as well as, focus on keeping balance of carbohydrates, protein and fat, along with vitamins, minerals, fiber and other nutrients.

High Blood Pressure

Avoid salted packaged foods, prevent taking in excessive sugar, reduce items high in saturated fats. People with high blood pressure can replace animal, saturated, and trans fats with plant fats. Eating high-fiber foods such as nuts, beans, and whole grains is also helpful.

Religious Aspect[4]

There are many different types of religious dietary laws. We have included a summary of what we determined to be the most applicable religious based diets to be included in our application. These were chosen based on their popularity and the uniquness of these diets.

Hinduism

A lacto vegetarian diet is followed by many Hindus – no meat, poultry or fish, no eggs, but milk products are allowed and encouraged. Beef is prohibited. Brahmins may have restrictions on who prepares their food and how it is stored. There are many fasting days and periods in the calendar, with restrictions such as eating only plant foods.

Islam

Meats should be slaughtered under Halal guidance; pork is not allowed. Alcohol is also banned under Halal. During Ramadan, there comes more guidelines for fasting.

Judaism/Kosher

This extremely complex set of guidelines includes restrictions on how meat is slaughtered, which animals/birds/seafood may be eaten (most famously pork and shellfish are not allowed), the part of the animal that can be eaten, who makes certain foods, combinations of foods, avoiding contamination, what can be eaten on religious holidays, and more. Such as, on religious holidays, it’s best to purchase pre-packaged kosher meals and leave them unopened to ensure the attendee can see there hasn’t been any contamination.

Mormonism

Mormonism prohibits alcohol, caffeine tobacco and illegal drugs(in coffee, tea, chocolate etc.). In addition, Mormons participate in a show of self-control and humility through a monthly fast, equating to missing two meals, on the first Sunday of each month.

Ethical Aspect

"Ethical eating or food ethics refers to the moral consequences of food choices, both those made by humans for themselves and those made for food animals. Common concerns are damage to the environment, exploitative labor practices, food shortages for others, inhumane treatment of food animals, and the unintended effects of food policy. " -- Wikipedia[5]. They are calling consumers for making informed choices about the foods they buy and avoid foods derived from animals abused on farms, unsustainable farming practices or unfair working conditions. Their main concerns focus on animal welfare, the environment and workers' rights.

Plants are widely favoured. Preferences are nuts, grains, legumes, vegetables, and fruits. However, being as an ethical eating person, you are not necessarily to be vegan, intake of meat is still okay, just look for meat that is sustainable and responsible. Always look for good source of food, choose food with brand guarantee. And when eat outside, choose restaurant that has impeccable reputation can be more rest assured.[6]

Secondary Research Conclusions

Doing secondary research required a large amount of work. We found there was a lot of information available so choosing a direction and narrowing down the scope was the first tough task we faced. After gathering all the information we needed from credible sources, we needed to refine the content to make it more readable so it can be used as our knowledge base for future development. Personas

Vegan Blogger/Influencer Veronica

Follows a vegan diet because she believes it much more ethical than consuming animal-based products. She is a popular blogger who writes about her vegan lifestyle and encourages others to adopt a vegan diet as well. Veronica is twenty-five years old and lives in downtown Los Angeles. She also works as a fitness trainer so she has a very busy lifestyle. Veronica likes using various productivity and management apps to organize her daily tasks so a grocery management app such as ours which will focus on her vegan diet will be beneficial to her. Veronica will be able to manage her groceries and also find new vegan alternatives to food items and recipes which she can share with her followers.

Severe Nut Allergy Nick

Nick is an 18 year old university student who has a severe allergy to peanuts and various other tree nuts. The allergy reactions he suffers can be life-threatening so Nick avoids any food items that may contain any kind of nuts in them. Nick is living away from his parents for the first time so he will need to learn how to shop for groceries carefully and effectively. He may use our app to check whether certain products contain nuts, find alternative products which he can look for the next time he shops and find recipes which will not contain any kind of nuts in its ingredients.

Low-Calorie Christine

Christine is a busy, forty year old mom to three kids. She has a full time job during the daytime and is busy in the evenings with the kids. Christine has recently decided to make some time for herself and improve her health and fitness. Her goal is to lose thirty pounds by exercising and going on a low-calorie diet. This means that she has to put extra care and effort into making sure all the food she is buying and preparing is low on calories and sugar. Our grocery management might prove to be useful for a busy mom like Christine who can use it to manage all her groceries and most importantly identify which foods are low on calories and which are not. She will be able to track, shop and prepare food which both she and her family enjoy while ensuring they conform to her low-calorie diet.

Jewish Jared

Jared is a thirty-three year old man who lives in New York. He practices Judaism so he follows the Jewish customs in his everyday life including in the food he eats. This means he eats kosher food which conforms to the Jewish dietary regulations. There are various restrictions as part of the kosher diet which include avoiding pork and shellfish. Therefore, Jared might find it useful to use our app to manage his groceries and the food he eats. The app can avoid all the foods which are part of Jared’s dietary restrictions and will be able to suggest popular kosher recipes.

No Restrictions Nora

Nora is a fifty-two year old woman who lives with her family in Toronto. Nora does not have any dietary restrictions and is open to almost any product. She may occasionally want to look for some low-sugar options or a dairy-free recipe to make for her mildly lactose intolerant son. Nora may discover our app through the recommendation of a friend and decide to try it out as she would like a tool to help her track and manage her groceries, look for new food products as well as recipes.

Personas Conclusions

Creating the personas for our app was a fairly easy task. This is because it is complemented well with the secondary research from which we discovered various categories of dietary restrictions and what they entail. Using this information we could easily recognize several user categories which helped us shape our final five personas. These personas helped us visualize and characterize real-life potential users who will be using our app and allowed us to ensure that our app’s functionality serves all of these users. The one downside to using personas is that it may not reflect all potential users or all the needs of those users because we are creating these personas from our own research and understanding without actually talking to any real individuals.
